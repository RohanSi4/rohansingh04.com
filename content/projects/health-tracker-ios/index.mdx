I started this as a standalone Swift app for logging workouts and health
metrics. The useful question was simple: could I keep my own activity history in
a form I controlled and make it easier to understand than a collection of
screens inside separate fitness apps?

The app itself was archived after the idea moved in a different direction. A
lightweight iOS shortcut and webhook pipeline was a better fit for collecting
data automatically, while the website became the place to display it. That path
removed the need to maintain a full native interface just to move information
from a phone into a personal dashboard.

The original repository remains available as a snapshot of the first approach.
Its more important outcome is the system it led to: the live health and fitness
data on this site grew from the same desire to own the data flow and present it
in a way that feels personal.
